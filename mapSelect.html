Durga Puja celebrates the victory of the goddess Durga over the demon king Mahishasura.
It begins on the same day as Navratri, a nine-night festival in many northern and western states of India(majorly celebrated in Bengal and Assam) that more broadly celebrate the divine feminine(shakti).

Durga Puja’s first day is Mahalaya, which heralds the advent of the goddess (The Devipaksha starts ). Celebrations and worship begins on Sasthi, the sixth day.

The celebrations end with Vijayadashami (“Tenth Day of Victory” ), when, amid loud chants and drumbeats, sacred images are carried in huge processions to local rivers, where they are immersed. That custom is symbolic of the departure of the deity to her home and to her husband, Shiva, in the Himalayas. Images of the goddess—astride a lion, attacking the demon king Mahishasura—are placed at various pandals (elaborately decorated bamboo structures and galleries) and temples.

Lord Ganesha has many attributes, he is readily identified by
his elephant head. He is widely revered, more
specifically, as the remover of obstacles and thought
to bring good luck. Just her sister Saraswati he is
also good with knowledge and also wealth.
Goddess Laxmi is the goddess of wealth,
fortune and beauty. She along with 'saraswati' and 'parvati' forms
the 'tridevi'. Her iconography shows her with two hands
holding a lotus which symbolises fortune and the paddy she
holds symbolise the the good food production.
Goddess Durga is considered as the epitome of power and beauty. she is also known
'mahisasuramardini' for slaying the demon
king 'mahisasura' and 'dashasabhuja' with
her ten hands which carry ten
different weapons along with the 'trishula'.
Her astride is a lion offered to her by 'himalaya'.
Goddess Saraswati is the goddess
of knowledge and different forms of art,
music, dance, speech. Her iconography shows her holding
the 'vedas' depicting the knowledge and with the
another a instrument 'veena' symbolising forms of art.
Lord karthik is considered as the god of war.
Kartikeya has been an important deity in the Indian subcontinent
since ancient times, worshipped as Mahasena and Kumara
and Murugan. His astride is believed to be a peacock.
